First GPU, Geforce 256

The history of Graphics Processing Units (GPUs) can be traced back to the 1980s when they were first developed for gaming consoles and personal computers. In the early days, GPUs were mainly used to accelerate 2D graphics and later evolved to support 3D graphics rendering. However, it wasn't until the late 1990s that GPUs started being used for scientific and engineering simulations.

The introduction of programmable shaders in the early 2000s marked a significant breakthrough for GPUs, allowing them to perform complex calculations and simulations beyond just rendering graphics. This opened up a whole new realm of possibilities for scientific and computational workloads. The advent of parallel computing and machine learning further accelerated the development of GPUs, making them an essential tool for high-performance computing.
